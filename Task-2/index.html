Biography

Anuradha Koirala is a renowned Nepalese social activist and philanthropist, widely celebrated for her tireless efforts in combating human trafficking and rescuing victims of sexual exploitation. Born on April 14, 1949, in Kathmandu, Nepal, Koirala's journey towards becoming a beacon of hope for countless individuals trapped in the dark web of human trafficking began with a deep-rooted commitment to social justice and compassion. After completing her education, Koirala embarked on a career as a teacher, where she witnessed firsthand the vulnerabilities faced by marginalized communities, especially women and children. Motivated by a desire to make a tangible difference in their lives, she founded Maiti Nepal in 1993, a non-profit organization dedicated to providing shelter, rehabilitation, and support services to survivors of trafficking and those at risk. Under Koirala's visionary leadership, Maiti Nepal has grown from humble beginnings to become one of the most prominent anti-trafficking organizations in the world. Through its various initiatives, including prevention, rescue operations, advocacy, and legal support, the organization has transformed the lives of thousands of individuals, empowering them to reclaim their dignity and rebuild their futures. Koirala's unwavering dedication to the cause of ending human trafficking has earned her numerous accolades and recognition on both national and international platforms. She has been honored with prestigious awards such as the CNN Hero of the Year Award, the Ramon Magsaysay Award, and the Mother Teresa Award, among others, in recognition of her extraordinary contributions to humanity. Despite facing immense challenges and obstacles along the way, Koirala remains undeterred in her mission to combat the scourge of human trafficking and create a world where every individual is free to live a life of dignity and freedom. Her story serves as an inspiration to countless others, demonstrating the power of compassion, courage, and relentless determination in the face of adversity.
